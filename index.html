1. INTRODUCTION

Alora Macedon is a function centre with a beautiful event space, three guest accommodations, a pool, extensive grounds, and a separate ceremony shed located a distance from the main hall. We host weddings, corporate events, and private functions.

We are seeking a dedicated person or team to manage the day‑to‑day operations, maintenance, and event support. This is a critical role because we have a tight turnaround between events: guests check out at 10:00 am, and the next event arrives at 12:00 pm. We need a reliable, honest, and capable person who can either perform the work themselves or coordinate a team of subcontractors.

This document outlines our requirements. Please read it carefully and respond with your proposal, answering all questions in Section 6.

2. THE VENUE – QUICK OVERVIEW

  • Main function hall
  • Separate ceremony shed (located a distance from the main hall)
  • Three guest accommodations (each with bedrooms, bathrooms, living areas)
  • Outdoor pool
  • Gardens and grounds (including the area between the shed and main hall)
  • Kitchen facilities (used by caterers)
  • No commercial truck access for waste – bins must be moved by caddy to roadside

4. SCOPE OF WORK – WHAT NEEDS TO BE DONE

Below is the full list of services we require. In your proposal, tell us which services you will personally provide, and which (if any) you will subcontract.

A. CLEANING & TURNAROUND (Critical)

  • Clean the three guest accommodations, the main hall, and the ceremony shed after checkout. The window is strictly 10:00 am to 12:00 pm.
  • You must have enough people to clean all these spaces simultaneously, because the distance between the shed and main hall means you cannot do them one after another in the two‑hour window.
  • You must be able to handle sudden repairs or maintenance if something breaks during cleaning (e.g., a tap, a toilet).

B. WASTE & RECYCLING LOGISTICS

  • Because waste trucks cannot access the venue, bins must be moved by caddy to the roadside for collection.
  • You must manage general waste, recycling, commingled containers, and cardboard from all areas (main hall, ceremony shed, accommodations).
  • Ensure bins are at the roadside on collection days and returned promptly.

C. GARDENING & GROUNDS MAINTENANCE

  • Mowing, edging, weed control, garden bed upkeep – including the pathways and grounds connecting the ceremony shed to the main hall.
  • Green waste disposal.
  • Maintain a tidy, presentable appearance for events.

D. POOL MAINTENANCE

  • Chemical balance, cleaning, equipment checks.
  • Ensure pool is safe and inviting for guests.

E. EVENT SUPPORT

  • Open the venue (usually from 4:00 pm) and close after events. This includes both the main hall and the ceremony shed.
  • Bump‑in and bump‑out: moving tables, chairs, room configuration, and setting up the ceremony shed as needed.
  • During events: monitor toilets, spills, bins, and assist suppliers (caterers, DJs, celebrants) as needed across both buildings.
  • Be on‑site to let suppliers in and coordinate access, especially given the distance between areas.

F. HANDYMAN & MINOR REPAIRS

  • Small repairs such as fixing taps, tightening furniture, changing light bulbs in all buildings.
  • For larger issues, you would arrange a specialist (e.g., electrician, plumber).

G. COMPLIANCE & SAFETY

  • Testing and tagging of electrical equipment (or arrange a licensed contractor) for all buildings.
  • Fire safety: monthly checks of extinguishers, maintain evacuation plans, ensure fire alarms are tested – covering both main hall and ceremony shed.
  • Emergency management: we require a “cruise ship” level of preparedness. This includes:
    • Designated marshalling area(s) that account for the distance between buildings.
    • Fire alarm warning system and communication equipment (megaphone, walkie‑talkie) kept ready.
    • Regular drills with all staff (including kitchen and event staff), ensuring everyone knows how to respond regardless of which building they are in.
  • Non‑slip surface maintenance: ensure floors are cleaned properly to prevent slips in all areas.

H. SUPPLIER INSURANCE & RECORD KEEPING

  • You must collect a current Certificate of Public Liability Insurance from every supplier (cleaners, gardeners, caterers, etc.) who works at the venue.
  • Create a “per‑event file” with these certificates and any other relevant documents (e.g., test & tag records, fire inspection reports).
  • Ensure no supplier works without valid insurance.

I. ACCESS & SECURITY

  • Manage the venue’s access systems: remote disarm, pre‑access code validation, and fob entry for both main hall and ceremony shed.
  • Be on‑site to let suppliers in when they arrive, or provide them with temporary access codes. Given the distance between buildings, you may need to coordinate access for multiple parties at once.
  • Ensure the venue is secure at all times.

J. KITCHEN & FOOD SAFETY

  • If caterers use the kitchen, you must ensure they follow food safety requirements (they should have their own Food Safety Supervisor).
  • Coordinate with them so that kitchen operations are integrated into the venue’s emergency and fire plans.

6. APPLICANT QUESTIONNAIRE (MUST BE COMPLETED)

Please answer each question honestly and clearly. There are no trick questions; we just want to see that you have thought about the role.

Q1. How would you handle the 10:00 am – 12:00 pm turnover if there are three accommodations, the main hall, and the ceremony shed to clean? The shed is a distance away. Describe your process, how many people you would use, and what happens if a last‑minute repair is needed.

Q2. Waste trucks cannot access the venue. Bins must be moved by caddy to the roadside. How would you manage waste, recycling, and cardboard on a busy weekend after a large wedding, considering waste from both the main hall and the ceremony shed?

Q3. What is your experience with emergency management? Have you ever been responsible for fire drills, marshalling areas, or using megaphones/walkie‑talkies? Tell us how you would keep Alora Macedon ready for an emergency, taking into account the two separate buildings and the distance between them.

Q4. How would you ensure that every supplier (cleaners, gardeners, caterers, etc.) has current public liability insurance before they work on site? Describe the system you would use to collect and file these certificates.

Q5. The venue uses remote disarm, pre‑access codes, and fobs. You will need to manage these for suppliers and staff across both the main hall and ceremony shed. How would you keep access secure while ensuring that suppliers can enter when they need to, especially when they may arrive at different locations?

Q6. What is your approach to gardening and grounds maintenance? Do you have your own equipment? How would you ensure the grounds connecting the ceremony shed to the main hall are consistently well‑maintained?

Q7. How do you handle unexpected maintenance issues, such as a broken pipe or faulty lock, especially during an event or just before a guest arrival, and particularly if the issue is in one building while you are attending to another?

Q8. Tell us about your previous experience working with function centres, weddings, or similar venues with multiple buildings. What did you enjoy most, and what was the biggest challenge you overcame?

Q9. Are you available to work weekends and flexible hours according to the event schedule? Do you have any restrictions we should know about?

Q10. Is there anything else you would like us to know about your skills, experience, or the way you work?
